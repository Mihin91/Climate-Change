Solar Renewable Energy

Solar energy harnesses the power of the sun's rays to generate electricity or heat. This is done using solar panels (photovoltaic cells) or solar thermal systems

  • Photovoltaic (PV) Cells: These convert sunlight directly into electricity. When sunlight hits the PV cells, it excites electrons in the material, generating an electric current.
  • Solar Thermal Systems: These use mirrors or lenses to concentrate sunlight onto a receiver, which absorbs and converts it into heat. This heat can then be used to produce steam that drives a turbine connected to a generator.

Wind Renewable Energy

Wind energy converts the kinetic energy from wind into mechanical power or electricity using wind turbines.

  • Wind Turbines: These have blades that rotate when wind flows over them. The rotation turns a shaft connected to a generator, which produces electricity. There are two main types of wind turbines: onshore and offshore.

Hydro Renewable Energy

Hydropower, or hydro energy, utilizes the energy of flowing water to generate electricity. This is typically done with dams or other water control structures.

  • Dams and Reservoirs: Water stored in a reservoir behind a dam is released to flow through turbines. The flowing water turns the turbines, which drive generators to produce electricity.
  • Run-of-the-River Systems: These use the natural flow of a river without a large reservoir, diverting a portion of the river through turbines before returning it to the riverbed.

Geothermal Renewable Energy

Geothermal energy harnesses heat from beneath the Earth's surface to generate electricity or provide direct heating.

  • Geothermal Power Plants: These are typically located near tectonic plate boundaries where geothermal activity is more pronounced. They use steam produced from reservoirs of hot water found a few miles below the Earth's surface. The steam is brought to the surface, used to drive a turbine connected to a generator, and then condensed back into water and reinjected into the ground.
  • Direct Use Applications: Geothermal heat can also be used directly for heating buildings, greenhouses, and industrial processes.
